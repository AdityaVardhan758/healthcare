About Neurology

Neurology is the branch of medicine deals with disorders of the nervous system. The name “neurology” is derived from the Greek terms “neuron” (nerve) and “logia” (study of). All disorders and diseases involving the central and peripheral nervous systems, as well as its subdivisions, the autonomic and somatic nervous systems, blood vessels, and any sensor tissue, such as muscle, are treated in neurology. The area of neuroscience, or the scientific study of the nervous system, is significantly used in neurological practice.

A neurologist is a neurology specialist who is trained to diagnose and treat neurological conditions. Strokes, seizures, movement disorders like Parkinson’s disease, autoimmune neurologic illnesses like multiple sclerosis, headache disorders like migraine, and dementias like Alzheimer’s disease are all treated by neurologists.
