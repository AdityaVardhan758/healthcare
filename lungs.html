About Pulmonary

Pulmonary medicine is an internal medicine subspecialty concerned with the diagnosis and treatment of illnesses of the respiratory system, which includes the lungs, upper airways, thoracic cavity, and chest wall. General internists and other specialist physicians, such as internists practicing pulmonary medicine, handle the majority of respiratory issues. Pulmonologists are internists who specialize in pulmonary medicine. They aid in the diagnosis of undiscovered conditions and the management of tough, rare, or severe respiratory ailments. Pulmonologists specialize in structural, inflammatory, infectious, and neoplastic disorders of the lung parenchyma, pleura, and airways, as well as pulmonary vascular disease and its impact on the cardiovascular system, as well as the detection and prevention of occupational and environmental lung disease. Asthma, COPD, emphysema, lung cancer, interstitial and occupational lung diseases, TB, pulmonary hypertension, and cystic fibrosis are all disorders that pulmonologists examine and treat. Some sleep laboratories specialize in sleep-disordered breathing and offer diagnosis and treatment services.
