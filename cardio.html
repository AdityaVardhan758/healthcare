About Cardiology

Cardiology is a medical specialty that focuses on the heart and circulatory system problems. The name “cardiology” comes from the Greek words “cardia,” which means “heart,” and “logy,” which means “study of.” Congenital heart defects, coronary artery disease, heart failure, valvular heart disease, and electrophysiology are among the commonly occurring medical diagnoses and treatments available in this discipline.

Doctors specializing in this field of medicine are called cardiologists; who carry out tests, and are equipped to perform delicate heart procedures, such as heart catheterizations, angioplasty, or inserting a pacemaker in the heart. Pediatricians who specialize in this medicine are called Pediatric cardiologists. Physicians who specialize in cardiac surgery are cardiothoracic surgeons or cardiac surgeons, a specialty of general surgery. They treat heart disease-related specifically to the heart like cardiovascular diseases. Cardiovascular disease affects the heart, the blood vessels, or even both.
